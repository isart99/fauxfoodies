AROMAS & ATMOSPHERES

By Lucas Gómez-Doyle

The city is now cold. Tacos are warm. Frankly, the only logical decision to warm up the body is to eat tacos, so I headed to my favorite taco place in NYC, Los Tacos Numero Uno to indulge in a little self-care (scene: a cold Thursday afternoon). Most authentic taco shops are pretty fast-paced (eh?). When you arrive, the place (no matter what location you go to) is usually packed, so it's semi-important to know what you’re gonna order before you get to the cashier. Assuming you know what you’re going to get (which frankly you should because the menu isn’t exhaustive), you go up to the cashier, order, pay, and take the taco ticket they give you. Then you take the taco ticket and hand it to them. But, wait, Lucas, who is them?! Great question.

I don’t really know what the person is called the “preparer?” Food exporter? Person in charge of the tacos? Regardless, the ticket is given to them and you await your tasty treats. But wait, there is more. Next, and let’s end the debate and call them the taco maestro, because they are basically in charge of a whole crew of people, and the beauty they orchestrate is far better than say the New York Philharmonic (is this even a thing? I feel like old people just drop it in nonchalantly as an answer to “what did you do this weekend”), will ask in a matter of seconds “maiz o harina” (translation: corn or flour, which actually doesn’t make sense cause technically harina is flour but is often used to describe corn flour, so then it’s just corn or corn flour????? But I won’t fault the Spanish language). Once you make your choice, and really there is only one, unless you lack taste buds, the taco maestro will ask “con todo?” which obviously the answer is yes, because if the taco maestro offers, you accept.

The experience is fast paced, it's also filled with people who speak Spanish (which makes me feel at home), feels lively, and in an odd way the vibes (yes I know I am lame for using this word) is happy. This is probably because I am Mexican, so whenever I consume Mexican food I am reminded of my family, the love I have for them and the love I have for my community. Food constructs memories, identities, and so much more. Taco power.

Your job is now done, you can relax, if only for a moment. But it usually only takes maybe a minute or two for your food to finish which at that point you then have to find a place to eat. Here is the interesting part. At most of the Los Tacos spots, the places to eat are standing only, not sitting. But at Chelsea Market there are outdoor seating areas (from COVID or because, frankly, IDK because who takes that long to eat the spicy, hot, delicious goodness of a taco?). So, you stand and even if you are with other people, you take a moment, or moments of silence to eat. Dare I say it's almost a religious experience?

I usually have, and I did this time, two tacos de carne asada and one quesadilla de adobada (which I think is just al pastor?). LITERAL drugs. I obviously haven’t tried drugs (what are drugs?), but I imagine if I did, the experience would be comparable. However, I digress. Habanero salsa, a slice of pineapple to cut the heat, the fat from the pork in a salsa with the acid from the lime mixing (see what I did there, not saying I’m a good writer but….), ugh literally so good (I am salivating...gross). If you’ve seen the movie Ratatouille it’s basically like the scene where the Lil guy eats some cheese and strawberries and has a mental explosion cause the flavor is insane. Yes, you are the rat and the strawberries and cheese are the tacos.

BOOM, the experience is over, you have enjoyed your tacos/quesadillas/tostadas. You can now die happy, a belly full of delicious food. If you have not been, I would suggest going (they are vegetarian friendly too!). If you think there is a better taco place, I kindly ask you to fight me.

Before you order some, a word of advice from our favorite Mexican faux-foodie, Isabel Ríos:

How to properly eat your tacos…

Prepare to amaze all your Mexican friends by eating a taco so perfectly, they’ll book you a ticket straight to Mexico City. Order your tacos with the perfect, infalible formula: “(number of tacos) of (type of taco, no need to say the word taco) con todo, por favor”. Only say con todo if you want your taco with everything on it, which usually means onion, coriander, and salsa. The classic taco with everything is the pastor taco, which includes onion, coriander, pineapple, and salsa. The following steps are crucial to maximize your taco-eating experience. Hold the taco by its center of gravity (this shifts the more you eat it), and keep it horizontal (parallel to the ground). Tilt your head about 45º to consume it. Once you receive your delicious tacos, take a moment to remember your training. This is important! You may be tempted to keep your head neutral like a gringo, but this is a rookie mistake.

Listo, now you can claim your Mexican passport at your nearest embassy.
